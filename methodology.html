Research methodology

MIRP: structural refinement for musical reconstruction.

MIRP is the refinement layer designed for PyClef. It transforms raw neural detections into musically meaningful events by modeling the staff, clefs, systems and score geometry.

Staff modeling

Detected staves define the coordinate system used to infer musical height, system boundaries and page flow.

Clef-aware pitch

Vertical note positions are interpreted through the active clef, reducing the gap between visual detection and playable pitch.

System grouping

Braces and spatial proximity help identify musical systems, including piano-style paired staves.

MIRP flow

A deterministic layer after neural detection.

01

Normalize detections

Filter overlapping boxes and separate structural objects from musical symbols.

02

Recover score structure

Organize staves, braces, clefs and symbol columns to recover the reading order of the score.

03

Convert geometry into music

Use staff-relative coordinates to assign note names, octaves and event positions.

04

Export auditable outputs

Create annotated images and playback files so users can inspect both the recognition and the generated sound.
